01

The Mirror

Your agent sees a pattern you haven't articulated to yourself. It surfaces it when you need to hear it — not when you ask.

"You've been avoiding the financial conversation with your partner for 47 days. Based on your pattern, you delay big decisions when work stress is high. Stress is high right now. This one has a time component — rates are moving."

02

The Surprise

You open a new AI tool for the first time. Before you say a word, it already knows you. No setup. No explaining yourself.

"I see you've been deep in this codebase for 3 weeks. Your pattern says you're in the zone — I'll keep interruptions minimal. I know you context-switch between two projects, so I'll remember where we paused."

03

The Protection

Known notices when you're about to repeat a pattern that hasn't served you. Your agent intervenes — not by blocking you, but by telling you the truth.

"You've expressed enthusiasm about starting this new project. Historical pattern: 4 significant side projects in 3 months. Completion rate with more than 3 active: 25%. Consider sequencing rather than parallelizing."

A story

Will had been using AI agents for two years. They were helpful. But they treated him like a stranger every time.

Then he turned on Known.

Three weeks later, his agent noticed a pattern Will had never seen. He was systematically avoiding the one conversation that could change his career. Not because he was afraid — because every time he built up to it, a new project distracted him.

His agent said: "You've done this four times. The conversation with your CEO is the highest-leverage thing you can do this quarter. Everything else is displacement activity."

Will had the conversation the next day.
